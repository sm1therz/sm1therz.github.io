Argument Mapping: Rules

  • The Golden Rule
    • a simple argument
    • always has 2 Co-premises.
  • The Rabbit Rule
    • Any significant word or concept
    • that appears in the contention
      • of a simple argument
    • must also appear in one of the premises.
  • Holding Hands
    • Any significant word or concept
    • that appears in a premise,
      • of a simple argument
    • but not in the contention,
    • must also appear in another premise.
  • No Dangler Rule
    • Any significant word or concept
    • that appears in a claim (contention or premise)
      • Of a simple argument
    • must also appear in another claim.

All of these rules then mean:

  • In a well-structured simple argument:
    • every significant term or concept
    • must appear in at least 2 claims \ (contention or premises)
